Try Translator Intent Lattices (FDL)

Executive Summary

Tau Translator turns natural‑language intent into clear, testable specifications and back. It provides English → Controlled English → Formal Spec, deterministic validation, and assisted authoring with governance.

Trust & Licensing

No raw licensed Tau grammar is embedded or shipped. We operate at the semantics level and keep Tau printing as a user‑installed adapter. Knowledge packs contain only derivative summaries and examples.

Operating Modes

  • Assist‑Only: draft & refine; humans approve.
  • Generate Spec: propose controlled spec; deterministic validation gates acceptance and repair.

Architecture (High‑Level)

  • Presentation: PWA + Desktop (PyQt).
  • API: FastAPI endpoints for Prompt→Spec, Spec→Prompt, validation, translation.
  • Domain: IR, validators, policy; clean‑room GS/NSO IR.
  • Providers: local & external via an abstraction; BYOK supported.

Intent Detection & Analysis (What’s New)

We model ambiguity as a finite distributive lattice (FDL) over join‑irreducible facets (intent, guards, quantifiers, temporal). Candidates are bitsets; GLB via bitwise AND yields the “certain core.” Information‑gain clarifications operate on irreducibles. A clean‑room GS/NSO IR synthesizes controlled English. See the explainer: Distributive Intent Lattices.

  • FDL bitset projection (feature‑flag: TAU_OPTIMIZER_USE_FDL=1) — implemented.
  • GS/NSO clean‑room synthesis (feature‑flag: TAU_OPTIMIZER_USE_GS=1) — implemented.
  • Clarifications surfaced as “Clarify:” suggestions in the UI — implemented.
